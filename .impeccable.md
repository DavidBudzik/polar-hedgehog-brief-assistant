# Impeccable Design Context — Polar Hedgehog Brief Assistant

## Design Context

### Users
Startup founders and business owners working with Polar — typically at an inflection point (launching, rebranding, repositioning). They're excited but uncertain. They don't know what "brand strategy" means yet; they just know they need one. This tool is often their first touchpoint with the Polar team. It should feel like meeting a great strategist for the first time — warm, perceptive, and clearly good at their job.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: warm, purposeful, alive

The tool is an extension of Polar's voice. It shouldn't feel like software; it should feel like the agency. Pink `#EC008C` = energy, creativity, forward motion. Blue `#010C83` = authority, depth, strategic confidence. Together they say: "We're bold AND we know what we're doing."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Feel**: Pitch / Miro — creative tool energy, playful but professional, designed for collaboration. Not a productivity app, not a consulting template.
- **Theme**: Light mode. This is used during business hours, often in client meetings. It needs to feel welcoming and legible.
- **Anti-references**:
  - NOT consulting/deliverable — no dense tables, no PowerPoint grids, no document-like layouts
  - NOT generic startup SaaS — no glassmorphism, no neon gradients on dark backgrounds, no Typeform clones
  - NOT aggressive/flashy — the pink should feel confident, not shouty

### Typography Direction
Display font should feel editorial and have personality — something between a creative studio and a thoughtful magazine. Body font must be highly legible for form-filling, with subtle warmth. Avoid:
- Any font in the reflex reject list (Inter, DM Sans, Instrument Serif, etc.)
- Monospace as a "technical" shorthand
- Mismatched pairings that feel academic or corporate

Good candidates to explore: Bricolage Grotesque (editorial, confident), Chillax (rounded, approachable), Switzer (geometric humanist), Cabinet Grotesk (character without quirk). Pair with a readable body companion.

### Design Principles
1. **Conversation, not form** — Each step should feel like a thoughtful prompt being posed, not a field to fill out. The interface guides you through a dialogue.
2. **Guided confidence** — Users feel held and supported. Every step communicates: "We know what we need from you, and it's going to be fine."
3. **Polar's personality shows** — The tool has warmth and wit. It's not a neutral SaaS shell. The brand colors, copy tone, and visual choices all feel authored.
4. **Earned polish** — Quality lives in the details: consistent spacing rhythm, intentional typography scale, micro-interactions that feel precise. Not flashy effects for their own sake.
5. **Progressive revelation** — Don't overwhelm founders. Show the next right thing. Complexity is revealed as trust is built.

### Color Notes
- Primary: `#EC008C` (hot pink) — use sparingly for primary actions and key accents
- Secondary: `#010C83` (deep navy) — headings, strong text
- Neutrals: Tint slightly toward navy (`oklch` with low chroma toward blue hue) for cohesion
- Avoid pure white backgrounds — use warm off-white (`oklch(97% 0.006 260)`) for surfaces
- No pure black text — deep navy-tinted dark (`oklch(18% 0.03 260)`)

### Spacing & Layout Notes
- 4pt scale: 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64
- Step cards should breathe — generous internal padding (32px+)
- Strong left-right rhythm with clear reading column width (max ~680px for step content)
- Use white space to signal importance, not cards-inside-cards
